Scenario 1: Tariff Impact Analysis
Situation - The U.S. government suggests it will impose a 25% import tariff on Canadian manufactured goods in the next few weeks. We do not know when it will start or how long it will last. However, Nordstrom and its international vendors must have plans to execute when needed.
The Women’s Shoe department will be most materially impacted, and your Manager has asked you for an analysis and recommendations for current, mid, and long-term that target 40-45% Gross Margin.
Problem to solve – The tariff presents a significant additional cost. We need to understand the profitability impacts and develop suitable options to work together with the vendors on short-term solutions and have plans for longer-term ones should they be required.

Option Analysis
1. Nordstrom covers 100% of the tariff
2. Vendor covers 100% of the tariff
3. Cost share 50/50
4. Temporary trade discount - Nordstrom will still foot the bill for the full tariff; however, the Canadian vendor is open to offering a temporary trade discount of 10%
5. Raise Retail Sale Price
6. Blend of Cost Sharing and Raising Retail Price

Scenario 2: End-of-Season Inventory Decision-Making Analysis
Situation - The consumer luxury industry has been feeling pressure due to the combined effects of economic uncertainty and elevated interest rates. Sales in the season have not been as strong as anticipated/budgeted, and as a business, Nordstrom has been pulling all the levers it can to reduce inventory risk.
One of Nordstrom's most straightforward actions is cancelling orders in-season to receive less inventory. However, at the end of the season, Nordstrom is still left with $10.8 million more product than planned.
Problem to solve – What would be the best course of action to manage this inventory?

Scenario 3: Corporate Strategy Shift
Situation - Consumer behaviour has changed significantly in the past decade, and Nordstrom has decided that it is time to modernize some of its business strategies.
Historically focused on high-price, low-volume luxury retail, the company will now pursue a hybrid strategy. 
For example, Nordstrom has always focused on selling absolute luxury brands like Gucci, YSL, Chanel, Dior, David Yurman, and Christian Louboutin. 
However, it now wants to carry more aspirational and accessible brands like New Balance, Nike, Marc Jacobs, and Coach.
